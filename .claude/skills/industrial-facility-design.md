# Industrial Facility Design Skill

What separates industrial from typical commercial: scale, loads, process,
trucks. Apply when working on warehouses, distribution centers,
manufacturing, light industrial buildings.

---

## Use groups quick reference (IBC §306, §311)

| Use group | Examples | Key code drivers |
|---|---|---|
| F-1 | Moderate-hazard factory (paper, woodwork, dry cleaning) | Sprinklers usually required at modest size |
| F-2 | Low-hazard factory (beverage, glass, ice) | Higher allowable areas; less stringent FP |
| S-1 | Moderate-hazard storage (combustibles, repair garage) | Commodity classification drives storage rules |
| S-2 | Low-hazard storage (non-combustibles, parking garages) | Largest allowable areas; least restrictive |

Mixed-use (e.g. F-1 + S-1 + B office) is the norm. Separate by rated
construction or treat the whole building as the most-restrictive group.

## Site planning

### Truck circulation
- **WB-67** truck (53' trailer) is the standard design vehicle.
- Turning radius: 50' outside, 28' inside minimum.
- Dock approach: 130' deep paved area in front of docks.
- Trailer staging: 10×60' min per dock for parked trailers.

### Loading dock geometry
- Dock height above grade: **48-52"** (matches trailer bed at typical
  air-bag deflation).
- Dock width per door: **9-10'** centerline.
- Door type: vertical-lift sectional door, manual or motorized.
- Dock leveler: hydraulic, electric, or manual; rated by trailer
  capacity (35K/45K typical).
- Dock lights, fans, communications: by owner / process.
- Pit depth: typically 8-12" recessed slab.

### Yard / pavement
- **Heavy-duty pavement** at truck routes (8-10" PCC or 12-14" asphalt).
- **Light-duty** at car parking.
- **Concrete approach apron** at dock (50' min depth from face of
  building).
- Slope away from building: 1-2%.

## Building shell

### Clear height
- Bulk storage: 32-36' typical (allows 4-5 racking levels).
- General warehouse: 24-32'.
- Light manufacturing: 18-24'.
- Cross-dock distribution: 28-32'.

Clear height = underside of joist or beam to top of slab. Sprinkler
deflectors hang below.

### Structural system
- **Tilt-up concrete** panels on isolated footings: common for shells
  under 32'.
- **Precast wall panels + steel joist roof**: common at 32-40'.
- **PEMB (Pre-Engineered Metal Building)**: smaller spans; bid by
  manufacturer; fast.
- **Hybrid**: tilt-up + steel joist roof is the workhorse.

### Roof system
- **TPO single-ply membrane** over rigid insulation over metal
  deck on bar joists.
- Slope: minimum ¼" per foot per code (often higher for drainage).
- **Skylights**: 3% of roof area gives meaningful daylight; LEED IEQ
  credit.

### Slab on grade
- Thickness: 6-8" typical for trafficked areas.
- Capacity: 200-300 psf for racking; higher for stacked block storage.
- Reinforcing: dowel baskets at joints; macro-synthetic or steel fiber.
- Joints: 12-15' grid; sawcut within 12 hr of pour.
- Surface: steel-troweled; flatness FF35/FL25 for forklift areas.

## Racking + storage

### Pallet rack standard
- **Bay width**: 8-12' (3-4 pallets across, 48"×40" each).
- **Beam levels**: 8-10' clear between for typical pallets.
- **Aisle width**: 11' for reach trucks, 13' for sit-down forklifts.
- **Top of stack to sprinkler**: 18" minimum (per FM/UL).

### Storage classifications (commodity class)
Per FM Global / NFPA 13:
- **Class I-II**: non-combustible / mostly non-combustible products.
- **Class III**: combustible wrappers, plastic on pallets < 5%.
- **Class IV**: Class III + appreciable plastics (5-15%).
- **Group A plastics**: highest hazard; serious sprinkler design.

The commodity drives sprinkler density. Owner / process supplies the
classification; you design to it.

## Fire protection (NFPA 13)

### Sprinkler design density
- **Light hazard**: 0.10 gpm/ft² over 1500 ft² (offices, light retail).
- **Ordinary hazard 1**: 0.15 gpm/ft² (light warehouse).
- **Ordinary hazard 2**: 0.20 gpm/ft² (most warehouse).
- **Extra hazard 1**: 0.30 gpm/ft² (manufacturing with significant
  combustibles).
- **In-rack sprinklers**: required above ~20-25' depending on commodity.

Pump room + on-site water: required when municipal supply can't deliver
design flow at residual pressure.

### Egress in industrial
- F + S occupant load is low per IBC §1004.5 (100-300 NSF/occ).
- Travel distance: F-1 sprinklered = 250'; S-1 sprinklered = 250'.
- Exit doors: 36" wide minimum; panic hardware not required (low occ).
- Cross-aisles in racking: maintain code-required clear width.

## Mechanical (HVAC + ventilation)

### Conditioned vs unconditioned
- **Unconditioned warehouse**: heat only (unit heaters, IR tube heaters).
  Saves significant capex + opex; appropriate if process doesn't need
  cooling.
- **Conditioned**: full HVAC + RTU array on roof. Required if office
  occupancy > 25% of total, or if process needs.

### Industrial ventilation
- General exhaust per ASHRAE 62.1.
- **Process exhaust**: hood + ductwork capturing fumes at source
  (welding, paint, solvent, dust).
- **Make-up air**: balanced; locate inlets to avoid short-circuit.
- **Air changes per hour (ACH)**: 4-6 typical general; 10+ for
  process areas.

### Loading dock conditioning
- Air curtains at dock doors when open.
- Strip / brush seals at trailer interface.
- Dock-shelters where conditioning is critical.

## Plumbing

### Trench drains
- Required at: process areas, washdown areas, dock floors (if rain ingress likely).
- Slope: 1-2% to outlet.
- Cover: ductile iron or galvanized for forklift traffic.

### Hazmat / spill containment
- Diking around storage of regulated materials.
- Floor drains tied to oil-water separators or hazardous waste tanks
  per AHJ.

### Hot water
- Owner / process requirements drive sizing.
- Tankless or central tank? Process load determines.

---

## Electrical

### Power density
- Light manufacturing: 3-5 W/sf.
- Heavy manufacturing: 10-20+ W/sf depending on equipment.
- Cold storage: 5-10 W/sf (refrigeration).

### Equipment power
- Service from MV (15 kV) for large facilities; LV (480/277) for medium.
- Bus duct vs feeders depending on floor plan flexibility needs.

### Lighting
- **High bay**: LED 5000K / 6500K; 25-50 fc at floor.
- **Office areas**: 30-50 fc; daylight harvesting.
- **Outdoor / yard**: pole-mounted LED area lighting; full cutoff to
  avoid trespass.

## Code-specific gotchas for industrial

- **High-piled combustible storage**: ≥ 12' triggers special
  requirements (IFC Chapter 32 / NFPA 13). Plan + permits required.
- **Hazardous materials**: thresholds in IFC Chapter 50 for control
  areas; H-occupancy if exceeded.
- **Process safety management (PSM)**: OSHA 1910.119 for certain
  chemicals. Architectural / MEP impact.
- **Eyewash + safety shower**: ANSI Z358.1 — 10-second / 55-foot reach
  from hazard, tepid water 60-100°F.
